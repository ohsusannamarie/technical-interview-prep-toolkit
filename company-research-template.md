# Company Research Template

Good technical interview prep is not just studying algorithms. It is understanding the company, the team, the product, and the technical problems you may be walking into.

Use this template before recruiter screens, hiring manager interviews, technical deep dives, and final rounds.

## 1. What does this company actually do?

Plain-English explanation:

```text
This company helps [customer/user] do [thing] by providing [product/platform/service].
```

Notes:

- Who are the customers?
- What product or platform do they sell?
- What problem are they solving?
- Is this B2B, B2C, enterprise, developer tooling, infrastructure, hardware, AI, data, etc.?

## 2. What problem are they solving?

Write the pain point in human language.

```text
Before this product, customers struggled with...
This company makes that easier by...
```

Look for:

- Speed
- Cost
- Reliability
- Scale
- Security
- Automation
- Developer productivity
- User experience
- Data quality
- Infrastructure complexity

## 3. What technical domain do they operate in?

## 4. What does the role likely work on?

Use the job description to identify signals.

| Job description signal | What it might mean |
|---|---|
| APIs, services, distributed systems | Backend or platform depth |
| React, TypeScript, UX, accessibility | Frontend/product engineering |
| Data pipelines, ETL, warehousing | Data engineering |
| Model deployment, feature stores, monitoring | ML engineering / MLOps |
| RTL, verification, timing, PPA | Semiconductor / hardware engineering |
| Kubernetes, observability, incident response | DevOps / SRE |
| Architecture, roadmap, mentorship | Senior/staff scope |

## 7. Questions to ask the recruiter

- What interview stages should I expect?
- Will there be live coding, system design, or a project deep dive?
- What skills are most important for this role?
- Is there a specific language, tool, or platform I should be ready to use?
- How is the team structured?
- What is the hiring team hoping this person solves in the first 6 months?

## 8. Questions to ask the hiring manager

- What are the biggest technical challenges facing the team right now?
- What would success look like in the first 3-6 months?
- How does the team make architecture or technical decisions?
- What systems, products, or workflows would this person own?
- Where does the team need more depth or leverage?
- What kind of person tends to thrive on this team?

## 9. Questions to ask technical interviewers

- What tradeoffs has the team had to make recently?
- How do you balance speed, quality, reliability, and technical debt?
- What does the code review process look like?
- How are incidents, bugs, or production issues handled?
- How does the team measure engineering success?
- What is one technical problem here that is harder than it looks from the outside?

## 10. My interview positioning

Use this to tighten your story.

```text
The main reason this role fits me:

The strongest evidence from my background:

The technical story I should highlight:

The question I most want answered:

The concern I need to clarify:
```

## Fast version: 20-minute research sprint

If you are short on time:

1. Read the job description carefully.
2. Skim the company website and product pages.
3. Find one recent article, blog post, or announcement.
4. Identify 3 technical concepts to review.
5. Prepare 2 project stories that connect to the role.
6. Write 5 questions you actually care about.

The goal is not to sound like you memorized the website. The goal is to show that you understand the problem space and can connect your experience to it.
